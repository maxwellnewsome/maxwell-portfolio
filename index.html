Where hardware meets software.

I'm a Computer Engineering student at UBC (graduating May 2028) with a focus on embedded systems, firmware development, and hardware design. My work spans bare-metal C/C++, FPGA design, and real-time operating systems — I like working at the layer where code has real physical consequences.

Currently building firmware for AgroBot, UBC's autonomous agricultural robot. I'm drawn to projects that involve signal processing, control systems, and the careful craft of writing reliable software for constrained hardware.

Outside of engineering, I'm a lifeguard and swim instructor — I value precision, safety, and the ability to stay calm when things go wrong.
